# UX Principles

## Philosophy

Every page should answer one question.

Grow should reduce uncertainty for growers. The experience should help users understand what is happening, what matters, and what action makes sense next.

## Page Questions

- Home: What's happening?
- My Grow: How are my grows performing?
- Explore: What should I grow?
- Community: What are growers experiencing?
- Network: Who should I know?
- Learn: How do I improve?

## Navigation

Navigation should always reduce cognitive load.

Users should be able to predict where something lives before they click. Labels should describe product intent rather than internal implementation. Top-level destinations should stay stable as the product grows.

## Experience Rules

- Avoid duplicate experiences.
- Prefer progressive disclosure over overwhelming interfaces.
- Keep primary actions obvious.
- Keep secondary actions available but visually quieter.
- Preserve deep links and existing user paths.
- Make empty states intentional and useful.
- Make coming-soon states feel deliberate, not unfinished.

## Progressive Disclosure

Grow should reveal complexity in layers:

- Start with the answer or summary.
- Offer supporting evidence nearby.
- Allow deeper inspection when the user wants it.
- Keep advanced controls out of the way until they are needed.

## Trust

Trust comes from clarity, consistency, and honest context.

- Explain what data represents.
- Show confidence and limitations when relevant.
- Keep community-powered insights grounded in actual activity.
- Avoid overstating recommendations.
- Preserve user privacy and public/private boundaries.
