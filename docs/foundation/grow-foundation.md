# Grow Foundation

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Approved

## Purpose

Grow exists to help people learn, improve, and succeed when growing from seed.

The platform should preserve useful knowledge, connect it to trustworthy evidence, and return it to members in forms that support better decisions. Grow is species-neutral by design: existing plant-specific experiences may remain, but the foundation must support anyone growing any plant from seed.

## The Grow Promise

Grow will help members document what they do, understand what happened, learn from reliable evidence, and make better-informed decisions while retaining meaningful control over their identity and information.

Grow will distinguish clearly between self-declared identity, observed activity, validated evidence, and system Recognition. It will not present uncertain conclusions as established facts.

## The North Star

Grow should become the world’s most trusted platform and companion for people growing from seed.

Trust—not attention, volume, or engagement—is the primary measure. Product, data, and architectural decisions should strengthen that trust over time.

## Who Grow serves

- Growers
- Breeders
- Sources
- Educators
- Researchers
- Testing programs
- Community contributors

These participants may have different workflows, but they share a need for durable records, honest evidence, responsible knowledge exchange, and better outcomes.

## Core principles

### Create genuine value first

Build capabilities that materially help members learn, decide, document, or succeed. Avoid features whose primary purpose is activity without durable value.

### Trust is earned

Make authorization, privacy, provenance, eligibility, and uncertainty explicit. Preserve member control and avoid silent expansion of data use.

### Evidence matters

Prefer canonical, reproducible evidence over anecdote or presentation. Evidence volume and quality affect confidence; missing evidence must never be fabricated.

### Knowledge should be preserved

Session history, observations, images, outcomes, and validated findings should remain durable and reusable instead of disappearing into transient activity.

### Knowledge should be shared responsibly

Sharing must respect consent, privacy, attribution, evidence quality, and the intended audience. Public visibility and internal personalization are separate choices.

### Every grow contributes to learning

When a member chooses to contribute eligible evidence, each grow can improve personal understanding and the broader knowledge available to Grow.

### Better decisions create better outcomes

Grow should turn records and evidence into understandable context that helps members make practical decisions without overstating certainty.

### Value attracts everything else

Healthy participation, trusted partnerships, and sustainable growth should follow from member value. They are outcomes of a useful platform, not substitutes for one.

## The Grow Flywheel

1. **Document** — members capture sessions, conditions, actions, observations, and outcomes.
2. **Validate** — Grow applies canonical contracts, eligibility rules, provenance, and quality checks.
3. **Learn** — evidence becomes understandable personal and community knowledge.
4. **Apply** — members use that knowledge to make better decisions in future grows.
5. **Contribute** — approved evidence and responsible sharing strengthen the knowledge available to others.
6. **Improve** — better evidence improves future learning, guidance, and decisions.

The flywheel must remain voluntary, privacy-aware, and evidence-led at every stage.

## GEE — Grow Evidence Engine

GEE is the canonical platform terminology for the evidence layer. Its purpose is to transform eligible grow records into reproducible, appropriately qualified evidence while preserving provenance, sample size, confidence, and data-quality boundaries.

GEE must not manufacture data, treat low evidence as high confidence, or allow presentation code to redefine analytics. Legacy `gie_*` and `grow_intelligence_engine_*` contract identifiers remain compatibility-bound until a controlled additive migration can replace them safely; this document does not rename repository objects.

## Future engine direction

Future engines and services may support personalization, connections, recommendations, testing, learning, and guided experiences. They should extend the same foundation:

- consume canonical contracts rather than duplicate calculations;
- use only data authorized for the specific purpose;
- distinguish suggestions from verified facts;
- require member approval before suggested preferences become Grow Identity;
- keep Identity, Reputation, Recognition, and Evidence separate;
- provide safe fallbacks when evidence or consent is insufficient.

GPE, GCE, GRE, and GTE remain future integration directions. Their product and technical contracts should be defined in the phase that implements them, not inferred prematurely.

## Decision framework

Before adopting a significant capability, ask:

1. Does it create genuine and durable value for people growing from seed?
2. Is the underlying information canonical, attributable, and honest about uncertainty?
3. Does it preserve privacy, consent, authorization, and member control?
4. Does it strengthen trust across growers, Sources, breeders, educators, and researchers?
5. Is it species-neutral or safely extensible beyond a single plant?
6. Does it reuse durable foundations rather than create a competing architecture?
7. Can it be tested, migrated, and operated without weakening existing behavior?
8. Is this the correct platform phase, or should the concept become a Foundation Note?

## Long-term vision

Grow should become a durable knowledge and decision platform for growing from seed: a trusted place to document experience, preserve learning, evaluate evidence, collaborate responsibly, and improve future outcomes.

The platform should build once and extend forever where practical. New experiences should compound the value of canonical Identity, sessions, evidence, Recognition, and responsible connections rather than replace those foundations.
